Events In 2023-24

OWASP PCCOE

XSPLOIT Session 1

OWASP Student Chapter conducted its very first XSPLOIT session with the newly recruited team. The session covered the basics of cybersecurity for second-year computer students to address the growing need for cybersecurity education among aspiring IT professionals. With the increasing reliance on digital technology, understanding cybersecurity fundamentals is crucial for safeguarding sensitive information and mitigating cyber threats.

Cyber Sleuths 1.0

In an era defined by digital transformation, the importance of cybersecurity has never been more critical. Cyber Sleuths 1.0 is not just an event; it's a convergence of minds dedicated to securing the digital frontier. OWASP Student chapter conducted an event called Cyber Slueths 1.0 on 15 October 2023 as a part of the Engineer's Day celebration with a total registration of 148 teams. The event consisted of 3 rounds with teams of 2 members each.The event was not solely a competition but also an educational experience, it contributed to enhancing cybersecurity awareness and knowledge

CYBERKAVACH

OWASP Student Chapter and SIG CNIS in collaboration with IIC conducted a month-long observance called CYBERKAVACH as a part of National Cyber Security Awareness Month (NCSAM) typically observed every October. "Asuras on the Cyberspace is not a Myth" was the slogan. Under the month-long observance of CYBERKAVACH, numerous tech-related and nontechnical events were planned with a total of 1500+ registrations. Some of the events conducted were Cyber Security Roadmap, Adrishyam-the mythological escape the room, and CTF. We had participants from different colleges across Pune as well as international participants from countries like Africa, Saudi Arabia, Russia, China, etc.

Cybersafe Checkpoint

In an era where smartphones serve as indispensable extensions of our daily lives, the need for robust security measures has never been more critical. OWASP Student Chapter and SIG CNIS in collaboration with IIC conducted an event called Cybersafe Checkpoint on 13th October 2023 under CYBERKAVACH. This event aimed to spread awareness regarding the security of electronic devices including mobile phones and laptops among the students.

S.B Patil Session

In an era where the digital landscape evolves at an unprecedented pace, the need for fostering cyber security awareness among the younger generation has never been more pronounced. We strengthen our community against emerging cyber threats and lay the groundwork for a time when technology is used responsibly and securely by raising a generation of digitally literate and cyber-resilient people. As October is a Cyber Security Awareness month OWASP Student Chapter, PCCOE, and SIG CNIS in collaboration with IIC conducted a session with the baseline as “Cyber Security Essentials” at S.B Patil School, Ravet.

Pimpri Chinchwad University Session

In the ever-changing world of higher education, where both instructors and students are immersed in a digitally native classroom, cybersecurity awareness is essential to promoting safe and responsible online behavior. OWASP Student Chapter, PCCOE, and SIG CNIS in collaboration with IIC conducted a session at Pimpri Chinchwad University, Talegaon. The baseline of the session was “Cyber Security Essentials” but as it was conducted for engineering students it included practical implementations of basic tools.

Cyber Security Roadmap

The OWASP Student Chapter, PCCOE, and SIG CNIS conducted a Roadmap on 18th October 2023, which attracted 150+ attendees, held at the Mechanical Building Seminar Hall, the event featured speakers Mr. Atul Kulkarni and Mr. Kamaljeet Singh, who shared insights into industry trends, challenges, and career journeys. Mr. Kulkarni discussed current trends, while Mr. Singh highlighted educational and certification pathways for success in cybersecurity. The event provided valuable guidance and deepened participants' understanding of the field, thanks to the support of SIG CNIS coordinators Dr. Rachana Patil ma'am, Ms. Rucha Shinde ma'am, and Ms. Swati Jaiswal ma'am and OWASP faculty coordinator Mr. Ganesh Deshmukh.

Cyber Sleuths 2.O

It was a convergence of minds dedicated to securing the digital frontier. The event consisted of 3 rounds with teams of 2 members each. The first round consisted of a quiz that commenced at 8 am in online mode with 50 teams participating. The quiz had simple cyber security questions that enhanced the knowledge of the participants. The second round was conducted offline with 30 teams that were shortlisted. The second round focused on basic encryption methods and simple computer logic. 15 teams were shortlisted for the third round. The third round was cyber security scenario-based and consisted of 10 questions. The event was not solely a competition but also an educational experience, it contributed to enhancing cybersecurity awareness and knowledge.

Embark on a journey where the mystical and the modern collide in an immersive adventure like no other. OWASP Student Chapter, PCCOE, and SIG CNIS in collaboration with IIC conducted Adrishyam on 25th October with a total registration of 49 teams consisting of 4 members that delved into the unique experience of the Mythological Escape Room, an innovative concept that intertwines ancient tales with an escape room.

Questcon CTF - Cybersecurity Capture the Flag Event

Capture The Flag (CTF) events play a crucial role in enhancing students' educational experiences and skill development in cybersecurity. OWASP Student Chapter, PCCOE, and SIG CNIS in collaboration with IIC conducted Questcon CTF, one of the biggest events held in the series of events with over 400 registrations and participants from countries like Africa, Saudi Arabia, Russia, and China, and renowned Indian Institutes such as IIT Roorkee and IIT Varanasi.

NETWORKING SPRINT

The Networking Sprint sessions were designed to provide participants with a comprehensive overview of computer networks, from fundamental principles to advanced topics in network protocols. Participants were encouraged to ask questions, share their insights, and collaborate with their peers to enhance their understanding of networking concepts,the event consisted of two sessions.

Ethical Hacking Workshop

OWASP Student Chapter, PCCOE conducted Ethical Hacking Workshop for students with the aim of introducing them to essential concepts and tools in the field of cybersecurity. The workshop covered a range of topics like Phishing QR Code,Introduction to CTF, Kali Linux,Terminal Commands,Basics of Cryptography.

CTF Training Workshop

Building upon the foundational knowledge gained in both the workshops .The hands-on experience given to students inspired them to actively engage in challenges and increase their problem-solving skills. This immersive learning experience further solidified their knowledge and prepared them to tackle cybersecurity challenges with confidence and proficiency.

BYTE ME

OWASP Student Chapter at PCCOE organized "BYTE ME" as part of ANANTYA. In "Mission TARS Restoration," participants join Team Endurance on a space mission to save humanity. When their spaceship is sabotaged, they must reverse-engineer TARS, the AI helper, and decode messages to uncover the truth. Their objective: restore TARS and find a new planet for human survival.

SHERLOCK 2.0

OWASP Student Chapter, PCCOE conducted an event called SHERLOCK 2.0 under ANANTYA which was a sequel to our previous event SHERLOCK. The plot included Pimpri Chinchwad College of Engineering facing a severe cyber threat as hackers take control of all campus systems, displaying an anonymous video message. The hacker's message, "Got you surrounded! Así que rómpelo," signified their control. Despite efforts by the OWASP student Chapter, the hacker remained elusive. To combat this, they launch Operation SHERLOCK 2.0, seeking a skilled detective to unravel the hacker's identity and restore control. This event was conducted in 3 rounds.
